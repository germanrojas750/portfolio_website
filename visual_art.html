Artist statement

I am currently working on a series which explores the intersection between the aesthetic elements of the Southern Gothic literary genre and those of Surealism. I find the overlap between the two to be fascinating and powerful, with many of the dark elements of the works of Southern Gothic literature conveying the hopes, dreams, and psychological tensions of the unconscious mind of the Southern individual. As a foreign observer of the American South, I have always been fascinated with the history and dynamics of the region. The pillars which made up the surrealist ideology in the post-World War I world find themselves supporting the theatrical backdrop of the current sociapolitical movements occuring in the American South. The irrational, the revolutionary, and the poetic are at the forefront of Southern American expereince - and the secret war being waged against the forces of opporession only makes the juxtaposition of Surrealism and Southern Gothic more poigniant.
